Tips & Techniques

Show, Don't Tell

Instead of telling the reader that a character is angry, show the white-knuckle grip they have on their wand, the sudden drop in room temperature, or the way their breath hitches.

Writing Fundamentals

Kill Your Darlings

Even if a sentence is beautiful, if it doesn't move the story forward, have the courage to cut it.

Read Aloud

Read your dialogue out loud. If it feels awkward to say, it will feel awkward to read.

Active Voice

Use active verbs to keep the pace moving. "The dragon roared" beats "A roar was made."

Write Daily

Writing is a muscle. Even 200 words a day keeps the creative juices flowing.

The First Draft

Don't worry about being perfect; worry about finishing the journey.
